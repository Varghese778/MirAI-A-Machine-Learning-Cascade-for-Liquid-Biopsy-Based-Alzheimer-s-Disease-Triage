Biological Rationale Flowchart
Why this project exists — the AD biology that drives every design decision
Healthy Brain
Normal Brain Function
In a healthy brain, neurons communicate through synapses. Two key proteins are cleared normally:
Amyloid-β (Aβ) → Cleared Normally
Tau Protein → Stabilizes Microtubules
💬
"Amyloid-β and Tau are normal brain proteins. In a healthy person, Aβ is produced and cleared, and Tau helps maintain the structure inside neurons — like scaffolding."
What goes wrong in AD?
Alzheimer's Pathology
The Amyloid Cascade & Tau Tangle Formation
In Alzheimer's, Aβ clearance fails. Amyloid accumulates into toxic plaques. Tau becomes hyperphosphorylated ("pTau"), detaches from microtubules, and forms neurofibrillary tangles. Neurons die.
Aβ Overproduction → Amyloid Plaques → Synapse Loss
Tau Hyperphosphorylation → Neurofibrillary Tangles → Neuronal Death
💬
"Alzheimer's is driven by two toxic processes: sticky amyloid plaques outside neurons, and tangled Tau inside neurons. Both destroy brain cells years before any symptoms appear — that's the window MirAI targets."
How do we detect this non-invasively?
Liquid Biopsy — Blood Biomarkers
Plasma Biomarkers Leak into the Bloodstream
Brain pathology leaves molecular fingerprints in the blood. Recent breakthroughs (2020–2025) made it possible to measure these in a simple blood draw — no spinal tap needed.
pTau-217 / Aβ42 Ratio
The #1 predictor. Phospho-tau 217 rises as tangles form; Aβ42 drops as plaques trap it. High ratio = strong AD signal.
Aβ42 / Aβ40 Ratio
Aβ42 is the "sticky" form that clumps. When more clumps into plaques, less is free in blood → ratio decreases in AD.
NfL (Neurofilament Light)
Released when neurons are damaged or dying. A general neurodegeneration marker — rises in AD, MS, TBI, and other conditions.
GFAP (Glial Fibrillary Acidic Protein)
Released by astrocytes (brain support cells) during inflammation. Elevated GFAP = neuroinflammation response to amyloid.
💬
"These four markers are like smoke alarms — each detecting a different type of fire. pTau-217 detects tangles, Aβ ratio detects plaques, NfL detects dying neurons, and GFAP detects brain inflammation. Together, they give us a multi-angle view from just a blood sample."
What about risk factors?
Risk Factors
Who Is at Risk? Demographics & Genetics
AD risk is influenced by non-modifiable factors that MirAI captures in Stages 1 and 2:
Age
Risk doubles every ~5 years after age 65. The #1 demographic risk factor.
Education
"Cognitive reserve" theory — more education may buffer against early symptoms, masking underlying pathology.
Biological Sex
Women have ~2× higher lifetime risk, partly due to longevity and hormonal factors (post-menopause estrogen decline).
APOE ε4 Allele
1 copy → 3× risk. 2 copies → 8–12× risk. Impairs Aβ clearance. The strongest genetic risk factor for late-onset AD.
Why a 3-stage cascade?
Design Rationale
Why MirAI Uses a 3-Stage Cascade
Real clinical triage works in escalation — you don't order expensive tests for everyone. MirAI mirrors this philosophy:
Stage 1 — Free
Age, sex, education are already in the patient file. Cost: $0. Every patient gets screened.
Stage 2 — ~$100–200
APOE genotyping is a one-time saliva/blood test. Only flagged patients proceed.
Stage 3 — ~$200–500
Plasma biomarker panel (blood draw). Much cheaper and safer than PET ($3,000+) or lumbar puncture.
Alternative (Avoided)
PET Scan: ~$3,000–5,000. Lumbar Puncture: invasive, painful, $500–1,500. MirAI replaces both as a pre-screen.
💬 Key Selling Point
"MirAI saves the healthcare system from ordering expensive PET scans on everyone. It narrows down who actually needs further testing — like a funnel. Only the highest-risk patients get escalated, saving thousands of dollars per patient."
What validates this approach?
Biological Validation
SHAP Analysis Confirms Biology
We used SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) to ask the model: "What features matter most?" The ranking matches known AD biology perfectly:
#1 pTau-217/Aβ42 — Tau pathology (strongest AD signal)
#2 PTGENDER — Biological sex (demographic risk)
#3 NfL — Neurodegeneration
#4 Aβ42/Aβ40 — Amyloid burden
#5 AGE — Primary demographic risk
SHAP beeswarm plot — each dot is a patient. Horizontal position = impact on prediction. Color = feature value (red=high, blue=low).
💬 Explain To Judges
"We never told the model what these biomarkers mean biologically. It learned independently that pTau-217 is the most important — which is exactly what researchers found in clinical studies. This confirms the model is learning real biology, not statistical noise."
The bottom line
Mission
Early Detection Saves Lives
Alzheimer's pathology begins 15–20 years before symptoms appear. By the time memory loss is noticeable, significant brain damage has already occurred.

MirAI aims to catch the disease in its silent phase — using a non-invasive, affordable blood test — so patients and doctors can act earlier, when interventions (lifestyle changes, emerging drugs like Lecanemab) are most effective.
